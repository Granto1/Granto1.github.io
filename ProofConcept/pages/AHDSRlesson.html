AHDSR

What is it and how we produce it

what is AHDSR?

AHDSR is a common synthesizer tool used to manipulate the envelope of a sound. In music, envelope is a term used to describe how a sound changes over time. For example, pressing and holding a piano key leads to an near-instant initial sound that gradualy decays in volume to zero over time. This it the typical envelope of a piano key.


AHDSR stands for: Attack, Hold, Decay, Sustain, and Release

Attack: The amount of time it takes for the envelope to reach the end of that first stage, usually the peak level.

Hold: The amount of time the sound remains at its "peak" level

Decay: The amount of time it takes for the envelope to decrease to some specified sustain level

Sustain: The level of output while a sustain instruction persists (held note). It is important to observe that the sustain parameter is a measure of level, not time. This stage can theoretically last indefinitely (as long as the user continues to hold out the note).

Release: The time it takes for the output to decrease to zero after the key is released or the sustain instruction ends.
